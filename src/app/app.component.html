how it started for me

In my room, I have a drawer where I keep many of my childhood memories. A stranger looking at the items in this drawer would undisputedly conclude that the owner would become an engineer. At the center of these items, lies a book with its spine exposed, cover missing, and stained title page - visibly loved.

Blessed with an innate sense of immense curiosity as a toddler, this book, “Amazing Questions & Answers” by Anita Ganeri and James Field, was the only thing during that time that could quench my thirst for knowledge. As soon as I could read, I was spending countless hours reading and rereading factoids ranging from “Who was the first to fly?” to “What happens near a black hole?”. Each factoid was thought provoking, but I soon realized that not all factoids were created equal. I had a natural affinity for the questions about engineering and those inquiries would often lead to explorations beyond the book. I was born to be an engineer. Once I realized that, the rest is history.
